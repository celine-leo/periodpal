Hormonal Imbalance

Observations

  • General mood
  • Quality of sleep
  • Energy level
  • Concentration
  • Bathroom habits
  • Skin condition

Symptoms

  • Weight gain
  • Irregular periods
  • Heavy flow
  • Problems falling asleep
  • Acne
  • Tummy discomfort
  • Fatigue
  • Mood swings
  • Vaginal dryness
  • Discharge from the breasts
  • Hair loss/thinning
  • Cold hands and feet

When should I worry about my period?

Contact your healthcare provider if:

  • Have not gotten your period for three months or longer
  • Suddenly bleeding for more days than usual (period length is longer)
  • Bleeding much lighter or much heavier than usual (blood volume changes)
  • Experiencing severe pain during your period
  • Bleeding between periods
  • Feeling sick after using tampons
  • Think you might be pregnant
    • E.g., you've had sex and your period is at least five days late
  • Have not gotten your period within three months of stopping birth control pills and you know you're not pregnant.
  • Have any questions or concerns about your period or possible pregnancy.
